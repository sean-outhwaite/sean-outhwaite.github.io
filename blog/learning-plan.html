My Learning Plan

03 September 2025

Career Aspirations

In the long term I would like to become a Principal or Staff engineer (long way away, I know!) for a small to medium size SaaS company. I prefer IC roles over management positions, so am not super interested in pursuing a role in the team lead track. For the short term my goal will be to land a junior engineer position after completing bootcamp!

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths:

Problem solving - I really enjoy digging into problems and trying to resolve them. Especially when it comes to technical issues I've got a good sense of how to troubleshoot and resolve.
Ability to find information - Having grown up on the internet I know how to find and identify reliable information about a topic or problem.
Calm demeanor - I generally don't get frustrated or panic when something goes wrong, I expect this will happen a lot throughout my time at Dev Academy.

Limitations:

Strong desire to solve problems myself - This means I can find it difficult to ask for help, or sometimes spend too much time trying to figure something out on my own.
Need time to think things through - Not necessarily a bad thing, but I prefer to take time to think about things before making a decision, which can sometimes mean that others who are more decisive will make the decision first.
Don't always work in priority order - Although I'm pretty good at determining priority, I will sometimes choose to not start at the top and instead work on whatever is easier or more interesting for me.

Skills I want to develop while at Dev Academy?

Being more open in asking for help - I know that I'll learn more collaborating with others, so I want to work on asking for help rather than always trying to solve things on my own. I also want to develop my mentoring/leadership skills by helping others, so will be looking for opportunities to help peers when they are stuck
Forcing myself to do the hard work early and not leave it to the last minute - while I do get the work done, if it's something difficult or tedious I tend to procrastinate a bit which can create stress & anxiety. I want to build a habit of breaking things down into smaller tasks and tackling them instead.

My commitment to Dev Academy

During this programme I will be treating my learning like a full-time job. I will be dedicating 8 hours on weekdays and some additional time outside of those hours to revising and progressing my skillset.

While working with others I will exercise kindness, empathy, and respect. Treating them the way I would like to be treated.
I understand that everyone learns at a different pace, thinks in different ways, and comes from a different background. I will be open to different perspectives and tolerant of others values.

When things get tough, if possible I will take a break. Step away from my computer and take some time to clear my head. If necessary I will reach out to my facilitator for help.

How and where I will seek help

I'll start by timeboxing myself to find a solution on my own.
If I'm unable to do so in a reasonable timeframe, or if the problem is time sensitive I'll ask in the foundations-help-desk channel, with a thorough explanation of the issue and screenshots/code snippets if relevant.
If I'm unable to find a resolution there, then I will reach out to my facilitator directly.

What I expect from the Facilitation team

Feedback - it’s good to have an external perspective on where I could be improving.
Support - if I’m struggling I expect facilitators will be willing to help me. Not to fix any problems for me but to work with me to find a solution.
Respect - I expect that facilitators will treat me with respect as we work together.

External Commitments

The only recurring outside commitment I have currently is on Mondays, I need to drop off & collect my dog from daycare between 7:30-8AM and around 3-4PM respectively.
